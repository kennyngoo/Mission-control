Direction & Props

The Vibe

Classic 80s–90s American portrait studio. Earnest, slightly stiff, colour-saturated. The charm is in committing fully — no ironic distance. Every pose is deliberate. Everyone looks directly at camera.

  • Warm, slightly overexposed highlight roll on faces
  • Coordinated outfits — not matching, but tonally unified
  • Gradient backdrop as the hero element
  • Props used with confidence, not apology
  • Mix of seated / kneeling / standing levels in every group frame

Outfit Palette

Option A — Denim Tonal

Light to dark denim tones. White tee or shirt as base. Everyone in denim — different washes, same family. Very classic JCP.

Option B — 90s Brights

Each person wears a different solid colour. High saturation. Coordinate to avoid clashing — assign colours before the shoot.

Option C — Warm Neutrals

Cream, tan, camel, rust. Timeless. Works best against the Purple/Pink or Blue backdrops for contrast. Chunky knits welcome.

Power Pyramid

Front row seated/kneeling, back row standing. Structured levels. Hands visible. Everyone looks directly at camera with full commitment.

Shoulder Stack

Everyone stands side by side, outer arms around each other, leaning heads inward slightly. Formal but warm. Straight-on to camera.

Chin Props

All lie on stomachs in a circle or line, propping chins on hands. Exaggerated smiles. Slightly ridiculous — fully commit to it.

Playful

The Jump

Synchronised jump on 3-count. All leave ground at once. Arms up or out. Genuinely hard to nail — shoot burst mode. Worth the attempts.

05

Back-to-Back

Two people back-to-back, arms folded, slight smirk. Remaining friends arranged around them. Strong centre composition.

06

The Walk

Walking toward camera in a line, spread wide. Matching energy — either ultra serious or laughing mid-step. Gradient backdrop behind.

07
Classic

Seated Row

All seated on a bench or floor, leaning into each other. One arm around the person next to them. Relaxed, natural — let it breathe.

08

Piggyback Stack

One person on another's back. Rest standing around them. Creates instant height variation and genuine laughter without trying.

Lighting & Shot List

Lighting Setup

Key light: Large softbox at 45° — soft, flattering. Slightly forward of subject plane.

Fill: Reflector or second softbox at 50% power, opposite side. Keep shadows soft.

Background light: Aim a bare strobe or kicker at the gradient backdrop. Controls how deep the colour reads. Feather it — don't blow it out.

Colour temp: 5500K. Keep it neutral — the colour comes from the backdrop, not the light.
